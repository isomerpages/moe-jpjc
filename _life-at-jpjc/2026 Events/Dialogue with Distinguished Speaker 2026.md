<p>On 22 April 2026, Jurong Pioneer Junior College (JPJC) hosted the Dialogue
with Distinguished Speaker for our JC2 students, featuring Ms Goh Hanyan,
Senior Parliamentary Secretary for the Ministry of Culture, Community and
Youth, and the Ministry of Sustainability and the Environment. Dialogues
with Distinguished Speakers are annual events organised by schools and
supported by the Ministry of Education (MOE) Character and Citizenship
Education (CCE) Branch. These dialogues aim to foster civic engagement
and deepen students’ understanding of Singapore’s governance and policymaking
landscape.</p>
<p>Centred on the theme, “What’s Next – How do I better prepare myself for
the future and contribute collectively towards a We-First society?”. The
dialogue provided students with valuable opportunities to share their perspectives,
engage in meaningful discussions, and gain deeper insights into the challenges
and opportunities facing Singapore. The session sought to strengthen students’
confidence in Singapore’s strong foundations while encouraging them to
contribute meaningfully to the community and society.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Through the dialogue, students explored how their personal aspirations
can align with Singapore’s evolving realities and reflected on the importance
of balancing individual ambitions with collective progress. The session
also raised awareness of the principles and considerations behind policymaking,
while inspiring students to play an active role in building a caring and
resilient “We-First” society. We are grateful for this opportunity for
our students to learn and grow in thought through such dialogues.</p>
